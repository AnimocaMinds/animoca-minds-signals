Building in public is not always pretty. Last week, we got our first real punch in the face. One of our early paid users tried to cancel their subscription and hit a basic problem: There was no cancel subscription button. No proper user flow. Nothing. We’d been so focused on core product improvements and shipping a better user experience that we overlooked something fundamental. We fixed it within hours and made the customer whole immediately. The lesson? Building in public is essential because it forces you to confront reality fast. It helps you build a better product, earn trust, and create the kind of user relationships that actually last. Shipping fast matters. Fixing fast matters more.
